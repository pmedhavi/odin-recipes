lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering sheets of pasta with a rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and melted mozzarella. Typically baked until bubbly and golden, lasagna is known for its hearty, comforting qualities and is a popular choice for family meals and gatherings. The combination of flavors and textures—from the savory meat sauce to the gooey cheese—creates a satisfying and flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef and cook until browned. Drain excess fat.
  4. Stir in the marinara sauce, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, and half of the Parmesan cheese. Mix well.
  6. In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce. Place a layer of noodles on top.
  7. Spread some of the ricotta mixture over the noodles and sprinkle with mozzarella cheese.
  8. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of sauce and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  9. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
